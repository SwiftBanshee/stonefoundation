Packing Guide

On the right side of the screen you will see a list of items you need to pack. Please ensure that you have everything, as your entry into Canada may be impeded without the necessary documents.

Also note that power voltages and plug types differ between countries. For information on whether your electronics are compatible with Canadian outlets please consult this guide

Non-essential items that you may wish to bring include:

  • Something to remind you of home to reduce home-sickness.
  • A cellphone. Canadian phone plans are often cheaper if you bring your own phone.
  • An international Driving Permit (IDP) if you plan on driving.
Also make certain not to bring unnecessary items that take up a lot of space. For example, items such as towels, bedsheets and school supplies are cheap and easy to purchase in Canada. Also, food and drink may be taken away from you by international customs.

Banking & Budgeting

When living on your own, it is important to understand how much things cost, and to budget accordingly. The cost of living in Canada can vary, but as a general rule the following figures are a good starting point:

  • $300 for food
  • $650 for room rental
  • $150 for utilities
Of course, if you choose to stay in residence the cost will be between $1030 and $1148. This includes your room in residence as well as a meal plan.

When budgeting do not forget to factor in the cost of your studies as well.

  • $15, 600 to $16, 000 for a certificate/diploma
  • $15, 700 for a graduate degree
  • $21, 500 for a bachelor's degree
  • Between $400 and $800 for books
